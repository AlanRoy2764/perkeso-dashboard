What this page is. A moderating factor analysis: does the relationship between gender (predictor) and labour-market outcome (LOE / Placement) differ across education levels (moderator)? It pulls a joint cross-tab (quarter × education × gender) from the raw PERKESO records — the marginal long-format pages can't answer this. Each card isolates one education level: raw counts (M vs F, LOE vs Placement) on the left, female share on the right. Vacancy has no gender field, so it's excluded here.

Headline — does education moderate the gender effect?

Yes, sharply. Diploma holders are an outlier in both directions:

  • Bachelor & Master: roughly gender-balanced in LOE (≈48–50% female) but female-skewed in Placement (≈58–60%) — women are placed at a higher rate than they appear in job loss.
  • Diploma: male-dominated throughout. Only ≈36% of Diploma LOE claims and ≈49% of placements are female — about 12 percentage points lower than the other two levels.

This is the signature of moderation: if education had no moderating effect, all three cards would show the same gender pattern. They don't.
